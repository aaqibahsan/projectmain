Indigenous animals in Sri Lanka

RED SLENDER LORIS
The red slender loris (Loris tardigradus ) is a small , nocturnal strepsirrhine primate native to the rainforests of Sri Lanka. This is No. 6 of the 10 focal species and No. 22 of the 100 EDGE mammal species worldwide considered the most evolutionarily distinct and globally endangered.

YALA

Yala National Park in Sri Lanka, established in 1938, is renowned for its diverse ecosystems and abundant wildlife. Encompassing a vast area of approximately 130,000 hectares, the park features varied landscapes including savannah grasslands, monsoon forests, and lagoons. Notably, Yala is home to a rich array of wildlife, with highlights such as the Sri Lankan leopard, elephants, and sloth bears.

Wilpattu

Wilpattu National Park, situated in the northwestern part of Sri Lanka, is the oldest and one of the largest national parks in the country. Established in 1938, Wilpattu is known for its diverse ecosystems and abundant wildlife. The park features a mix of natural lakes, dense forests, and open grasslands, creating a unique landscape.
